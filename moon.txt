<p> When looking up at the night sky, the first thing you'll probably notice is the Moon! After all, the Moon is the brightest celestial object in Earth's sky only after the Sun.</p>

<p> The Moon has significant responsibilties. The Moon makes Earth a more livable planet by moderating our home planet's wobble on its axis, leading to a relatively stable climate. She also causes tides, creating a rhythm that has guided humans for thousands of years.</p>

<p>Our lunar neighbor has integrated herself in all aspects of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:Moon_in_culture">culture</a>. From the beginning of human history, the Moon has acted as a 'cultural mirror' to our beliefs, understanding, and ways of seeing. Over the centuries, the Moon has been interpreted as a God and a planet, as well as a timekeeper and calendar.</p>

  <br>  

<h2>Phases of the Moon</h2>
<p>Throughout each month, our view of the Moon cycles through eight distinct phases depending on how the Sun illuminates the side of the Moon that is facing the Sun. These eight phases are, in order:</p>

<ul>
  <li>New Moon</li>
  <li>Waxing Crescent</li>
  <li>First Quarter</li>
  <li>Waxing Gibbous</li>
  <li>Full Moon</li>
  <li>Waning Gibbous</li>
  <li>Third Quarter</li>
  <li>Waning Crescent</li>
</ul>
