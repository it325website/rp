<h1> - The Stars -</h1>

<p> Another twinkling object you might find in the night (or even day!) sky are stars. <dfn>Stars</dfn> are huge celestial bodies made mostly of hydrogen and helium that produce light and heat from the churning nuclear forges inside their cores. The dots of light we see in the sky are all light-years from Earth, except for the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun">Sun</a> which is the nearest star to Earth.</p>


<h2>Constellations</h2>
<p>A constellation is an area on the celestial sphere in which a group of visible stars forms a perceived outline or pattern, typically representing an animal, mythological person or creature, or an inanimate object. The origins of the earliest constellations likely go back to prehistory. People used them to relate stories of their beliefs, experiences, creation, or mythology.</p>

<p>Examples of well-known constellations include: </p>
<ul>
  <li>Cassiopeia</li>
  <li>Orion's Belt</li>
  <li>Big Dipper</li>
  <li>Little Dipper</li>
</ul>

<h2>Zodiac Constellations</h2>

<p>Zodiac constellations lie along the plane of the ecliptic. The ecliptic, or the apparent path of the Sun, is defined by the circular path of the Sun across the sky as seen from Earth. In other words, the Sun appears to pass through these constellations over the course of a year.</p>

<p>There are 12 constellations in the zodiac family. When most people talk about their zodiac sign, they are most often referring to their Sun sign. Your Sun sign is calculated using the day you were born.</p>
